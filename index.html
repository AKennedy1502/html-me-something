Colorado Summer Vacation

August 2019

My husband and I spent our honeymoon in Colorado in 2012 and we wanted to introduce our six year old son, Riley, to the beauty of "The Centennial State" this past summer.

Denver

We arrived in Denver after a long 10 hour drive and checked into our bargain motel. We looked around for a close restaurant to have dinner at. We love unique places that are kid friendly so we were immediately drawn to a restaurant that offered food, bowling, karoke and was housed in the 27-story former air traffic control tower of the Stapleton International Airport. Punch Bowl Social had taken over the closed tower and re-imagined it into a fun and visually inspiring retro "eater-tainment" concept.

While in Denver, we also visited Casa Bonita, the tourist trap restaurant that has been open for 40 years. It features a 30-foot waterfall and indoor cliff divers that put on a show every 15 minutes.

Estes Park

A drive along the front range led us to Estes Park and the Aerial Tramway. It has been open since 1955 and is one of the few free span trams in the United States. We enjoyed a smooth and steady 3.5 minute ride up the 1,100 vertical feet to the top of Prospect Mountain. We bought peanuts at the small cáfe at the top to feed to the resident chipmonks and enjoyed the breathtaking views of Estes Park.

Colorado Springs

We previously drove to Colorado Springs in 2012 just after they had experienced the Waldo Canyon fire . Back then you could see blackened areas of devastation from the highway. It was good to see the regrowth and new construction in the same area this summer. We drove up 14,115 feet to the top of Pikes Peak and then enjoyed some special high-elevation recipe donuts as we looked around.

After the somewhat harrowing drive down Pikes Peak, we drove over to the Garden of the Gods to see the amazing red sandstone formations.

South Dakota

With an extra day left on our vacation with no particular plans, we decided to pack up a day early and take a longer route on the way home in order to see Mt. Rushmore in South Dakota. We drove north through Colorado and through the corner of Wyoming and then on to South Dakota. We drove close enough to see the ongoing transformation of the Crazy Horse Monument but we did not stop.

We realized that the Sturgis motorcycle rally was happening when we saw an unsual amount of motorcycles joining us on our drive to Keystone, SD, to the Mount Rushmore National Memorial. Riley was very excited to see the monument because he had studied Abraham Lincoln in his Kindergarten class. We finally got back into our car and continued on our way.

We stopped at a rest stop in Chamberlain, SD, and happened upon an amazing work of art. The breathtaking 50-foot statue is named "Dignity."

Home

Riley fell asleep in the car and we decided to just keep driving. We arrived home at 3:30am, twenty hours after we started our journey home from Colorado. What an amazing vacation!
